The Bund (2 hours):Situated in the eastern part of Huangpu District, Shanghhai, the Bund runs along the western bank of the Huangpu River, centres on a section of Zhongshan Road. At the west side of the Bund, there are 26 buildings in diverse architectural styles, such as Baroque,Gothic, Classicism, Romanesque and the Renaissance etc.

Day 2 Wed 22-May-13

Place & Transport: Shanghai
Accommodation: Shanghai Holiday Inn Express Hotel Zhaibei
Today's Activities: Yuyuan Garden,City God Temple,Shanghai Museum,Xin Tian Di,People Square,Oriental Pearl TV Tower,Shanghai Circus
Meals: Breakfast served in hotel,Chinese Lunch

Oriental Pearl TV Tower (1 hour):Situated in Pudong Park in Lujiazui, Shanghai, Oriental Peaarl TV Tower is the world's third tallest TV tower with a hight of 468 meters only after towers in Toronto, Canada and Moscow, Russia.

Yuyuan Garden (1.5 hours):Situated in the Anren Street of Shanghai and covering an area of about 20,000 square meters, Yuyuan Garden was finished in 1,577 by a government officer named Pan Yunduan for his parents as a place for them to enjoy a quiet and happy time in their old age and now Yuyuan Garden has become a popular place for both the locals and tourists.

City God Temple (50 minutes):Situated in Huangpu area nearby Yuyuan Market, City God Temple was constrtuced in the reign of Yongle Emperor in the Ming Dynasty (1,368 to 1,644). There are many companies around it, and it is said that here is the original bustling areas in Shanghai. The local specialties selling here attracts lots of visitors and businessmen from all over the world every year.

Xin Tian Di (50 minutes ):Situated in the center of Shanghai, south of Huaihai Zhong Road, Shanghai Xin Tian Di is a comprehensive business area of eating, shopping and recreation. It consists of an area of reconstituted traditional Shikumen ("stone gate") houses on narrow alleys, some adjoined book stores houses, shopping malls and cafes and restaurants. Additionally, there are both indoor and outdoor seatings in most of the cafes and restaurants, very westernized.

People Square (40 minutes):Situated in Huangpu District, Shanghai, Shanghai People Square covers an area of about 140,000 square meters. It is made up of an open square, the People's Park and some distinctive buildings. Nowadays, it has become one of the most popular and important landmarks in Shanghai.

Shanghai Circus (1.5 hours):Shanghai Circus is quite famous for its wonderful acrobatics, magic, dancing and music performances in the world. Situated at Gonghe Xin Road,Shanghai, Shanghai Circus World is known as "China's No 1 Circus World".

Shanghai Museum (2 hours):Located on the People's Square in the Huangpu District of Shanghai, Shanghai Museum is a museum of ancient Chinese art built in 1,952. The original site of the museum was in the Former Shanghai Racecourse club house, moved into the Former Zhonghui Building in 1,600, then, andmoved to People's Square finally .

Xitang Water Town (1 day):Xitang Water Town is located in Jiashan County in Jiaxing City of Zhejiang Province. It is the cradle of Wu & Yue culture. In the town, there are well-preserved groups of buildings of the Ming Dynasty (1368-1644) and the Qing Dynasty (1644-1911).

Day 4 Fri 24-May-13

Place & Transport: Xian
Accommodation: Xian City Hotel
Today's Activities: Terra Cotta Warriors and Horses Museum,Big Wild Goose Pagaoda,Tang Dynasty Music and Dance Show,Dumplings Feast
Meals: Breakfast served in hotel,Chinese Lunch

Terra Cotta Warriors and Horses Museum (3 hours):The No.1 Attraction in Xian of China, Terra Cotta Warriors and Horses describes the fabulous Qin Dynasty and its first Emperor Qinshihuang to the public with nearly 7,000 life-size pottery soldiers, chariots, weapons and horses.

Big Wild Goose Pagaoda (2 hours):Situated in the southern suburb of Xian, China, Big Wild Goose Pagoda or Dayan Pagoda is a famous ancient construction and a holy place for Buddhists and attracts countless tourists from all over the world for its status in the Buddhist religion.

Dumplings Feast (1 hour):Xian delectable dumplings is a must-eat for you while travelling in Xian. While tasting those, you will be shocked by the cooks' cooking methods include pan-frying,steaming, boiling,  deep frying and roasting. Additionally, it seems that almost all of the vegetables  and  indigenous meat can be used as the dumplings' stuffing.

Tang Dynasty Music and Dance Show (1.5 hours):Tang Dynasty is considered as the most progressive and booming ear in the history of ancient China, as well as its graceful dance and fair-sounding music. Situated at No. 75 Chang'an Lu, Xian, the Tang Dynasty Theatre Restaurant will offer the best service for you.

Day 5 Sat 25-May-13

Place & Transport: Xian-Beijing
Accommodation: No
Today's Activities: Xian City Wall,Shaanxi Historical Museum,Great Mosque,Muslim Street. Transfer to Xian airport. Fly to Beijing. Your tour ends.
Meals: Breakfast served in hotel,Chinese Lunch

Xian City Wall (3.5 hours):Xian City Wall is the most complete city wall existig in China, as well as one of the largest ancient military self-defensive systems in the world. It is constructed originally in the early Ming Dynasty (1368-1644).

Great Mosque (1.5 hours):Situated to the northwest of the Drum Tower, Xian, Great Mosque is one of the largest, oldest and best preserved mosques in China. According to exact historical records engraved on a stone tablet inside the Great Mosque, Great Mosque was originally constructed in 742 during the Tang Dynasty (618-907).

Muslim Street (1 hour):All restaurants and shops in Muslim Street are managed by Muslims, and the stalls sell different kinds of items. If you are good at bargaining, you will get some products at a quite reasonable price; if you are not, you should also have a try.

Shaanxi Historical Museum (2 hours):Situated in the southern suburb of Xian City, northwest of the Big Wild Goose Pagoda, Shaanxi Historical Museum is known as the showcase of ancient culture of Shaanxi Province.

China Tourist Visa Application:

Normally speaking, visitors with regular passport from USA, UK, Canada, Australia, Germany, French etc. to China will only require a tourist visa which will allow them to travel freely in most parts of China as tourists or to visit or accompany Chinese family members. Please make sure that you have got your valid passport and visa before your entry to China. We can send you a free official invitation letter to assist you in obtaining your visa after the reservation deposit is received.

Requirements
1Applicant' passport with at least 6 months remaining validity and available blank pages;
2One truly completed Visa Application Form. If someone else traveling with you shares the same passport, or if you are applying for a visa in a country or territory other than the country of your current nationality, you should fill out the Supplementary Visa Application Form. Please fill in every column of the application form, using "N/A" if not applicable. If the application form is not filled out truly, completely and legibly, this can cause a delay in processing or refusal of the requested visa.
3 A recent passport-size color photo with white background stuck on the application form. For tourism, applicants also need provide the below required documents.
4Under some situations, the original and copy of Chinese Hotel Booking and Return Air Ticket are required.
5For applicants who will travel into Tibet, they must obtain a Tibet Travel Permit. This can either be obtained by joining a group or by obtaining the permit from Tourism Bureau of Tibet Autonomous Region (Tel +86 891 6834313 or Fax +86 891 6834632).

How to Apply
You can come to the Chinese Embassies, Consulates and other Chinese diplomatic missions to submit application in person; if you can't come personally, you can entrust someone else, a travel agency or a visa agency to act on your behalf. Applications by mailing are not acceptable and will be refused at most of Chinese embassies or consulates. No appointment is required.

Processing Time
It regularly takes 4 working days for processing. For express service, additional fees of US$ 20 will be charged per visa for second or third working day releasing. For rush service, additional fees of US$30 per visa will be charged for the same working day releasing.
