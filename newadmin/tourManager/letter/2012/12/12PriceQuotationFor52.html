Xiushui Street (2 hours):Xiushui Street was originally filled with an marvelous arrays of name-brand bags and clothes and some of the best silk products. Nowadays, Xiushui Street still focus on pearls, jades, silk products, and silver and golden products, but not Xiushui Street any more, its a Xinshui Shopping Mall now.  

Wangfujing Street (3 hours):Located to the east side of Forbidden City, Wangfujing Street is one of most fashionable and the largest pedestrian streets in China. There are many shopping malls, stalls, and outdoor cafes during the summer - a wonderful place to take frozen beer or an ice-cream under a shady umbrella along with shopping.

Ox Street Mosque (1 Hour):Situated in Xuanwu District of Beijing City, Ox Street Mosque (Niujie Mosque) is the biggest and oldest mosque in Beijing, and is the spiritual center for the 10,000 Muslims living in the vicinity. Ox Street Mosque was built in 996 AD during the Liao Dynasty (916-1125), and rebuilt in 1442 in the Ming Dynasty and expanded in 1696 under the Qing Dynasty.

Art Ceramics Factory (40 Minutes):Inside the Xi’an Art Ceramics and Lacquer Factory. These larger than life sized terracotta figures are replicas of figures in the terracotta army. The emperor is the imposing figure in the middle of the group, while figures sporting two knots on their heads are generals.

Day 4 Wed 31-Dec-69

Place & Transport:
Accommodation:
Today's Activities: Humble Administrator Garden,Grand Canal ,Tiger Hill,Zhouzhuang,Sea Pearl Museum,Grand Canal,Lingering Garden
Meals: B,L

Humble Administrator Garden (2 hours):Covering an area of about 52,000 square meters, Suzhou Humble Administrator's Garden is the largest one in Suzhou and famous for its distinctive design and elegant beauty.

Lingering Garden (1.5 hours):Situated outside Changmen Gate in Suzhou of Jiangsu Province, Lingering Garden is one of the four most famous gardens in China with the typical Qing style. It is famous for its elegant beauty of imposing halls, and the diverse shapes, sizes and color of the buildings. Lingering Garden was recorded on the list of the World Heritage by UESCO in 1997.

Grand Canal (1 hour):Grand Canal is a great water conservancy project built in Sui Dynasty(518-618), which connected Beijing in the north and Hangzhou in the south and measures about 1,700 kilometers in length.

Tiger Hill (2 hours):Tiger Hill is a large hillock with a height of 36 kilometres and covers an area of about 14,100 square meters. While climbing the hill, you will find some historical relics which can date back to more than 2,500 years ago. Although Tiger Hill is relatively small, it has many wonderful attractions indeed.

Zhouzhuang (1 day):Zhouzhuang, one of the most famous water townships in China, situated in Kunshan City which is only 30 kilometers (18 miles) southeast of Suzhou. It is noted for its profound cultural background, the well preserved ancient residential houses, the elegant watery views and the strong local colored traditions and customs.

Sea Pearl Museum (1 Hour):Located to the south of Reed Flute Cave, on the bank of Peach River, South China Pearl Museum is a professional pearl raising , machining and selling  company. In this 10,000 square meters large museum, you could see the history of south pearl in China, and purchase 2000 types of ornaments made of south pearl.

Grand Canal (2 hours):The Grand Canal(1,764 km) is the longest man-made waterway as well as the greatest in ancient China. The Grand Canal contributed to ensure that the Chinese primary economy thrived in past dynasties greatly. Before it was linked together by the Sui Dynasty (581-618), The canal we see today was built section by section in different areas and dynasties.

China Tourist Visa Application:

Normally speaking, visitors with regular passport from USA, UK, Canada, Australia, Germany, French etc. to China will only require a tourist visa which will allow them to travel freely in most parts of China as tourists or to visit or accompany Chinese family members. Please make sure that you have got your valid passport and visa before your entry to China. We can send you a free official invitation letter to assist you in obtaining your visa after the reservation deposit is received.

Requirements
1Applicant' passport with at least 6 months remaining validity and available blank pages;
2One truly completed Visa Application Form. If someone else traveling with you shares the same passport, or if you are applying for a visa in a country or territory other than the country of your current nationality, you should fill out the Supplementary Visa Application Form. Please fill in every column of the application form, using "N/A" if not applicable. If the application form is not filled out truly, completely and legibly, this can cause a delay in processing or refusal of the requested visa.
3 A recent passport-size color photo with white background stuck on the application form. For tourism, applicants also need provide the below required documents.
4Under some situations, the original and copy of Chinese Hotel Booking and Return Air Ticket are required.
5For applicants who will travel into Tibet, they must obtain a Tibet Travel Permit. This can either be obtained by joining a group or by obtaining the permit from Tourism Bureau of Tibet Autonomous Region (Tel +86 891 6834313 or Fax +86 891 6834632).

How to Apply
You can come to the Chinese Embassies, Consulates and other Chinese diplomatic missions to submit application in person; if you can't come personally, you can entrust someone else, a travel agency or a visa agency to act on your behalf. Applications by mailing are not acceptable and will be refused at most of Chinese embassies or consulates. No appointment is required.

Processing Time
It regularly takes 4 working days for processing. For express service, additional fees of US$ 20 will be charged per visa for second or third working day releasing. For rush service, additional fees of US$30 per visa will be charged for the same working day releasing.
